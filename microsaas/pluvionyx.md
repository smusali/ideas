# **Pluvionyx — Simple Weather Context Annotator**

*A web app that adds weather and environmental facts to any text automatically. Paste an article, story, or journal entry, and get it annotated with relevant weather data for the time and place mentioned. Perfect for journalists, writers, and researchers who want to add environmental context.*

---

## 1. Executive Summary

**Pluvionyx** is a simple weather context tool that enhances any text with environmental facts. Paste your content, and it automatically detects dates and locations, then adds relevant weather information as inline annotations. No manual research needed - just paste text and get weather-enhanced content in seconds.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

People struggle with adding weather context because:
- **Missing environmental details** - Stories lack weather background that adds depth
- **Manual research** - Takes hours to look up weather data for specific dates/locations
- **Inconsistent data** - Different sources provide conflicting information
- **Time consuming** - Writers spend more time researching than writing
- **No simple tools** - Existing weather APIs require technical skills

---

## 3. Solution Overview

Pluvionyx provides instant weather annotation:
1. **Paste Text** - Upload or paste any content
2. **Detect Context** - Find dates and locations automatically
3. **Add Weather** - Insert relevant weather facts
4. **Download** - Get annotated text ready to use

**MVP Features:**
- Text input (paste or upload)
- Date and location detection
- Weather data lookup
- Inline annotation insertion
- Export to text/PDF
- No account required for basic use

---

## 4. User Workflow

1. **Input** - Paste article, story, or any text
2. **Process** - Automatic detection of dates and places
3. **Annotate** - Weather facts added inline
4. **Download** - Get enhanced text ready to use

**Example Results:**
```
Original: "The parade in New York on March 15, 2023 was spectacular."

Annotated: "The parade in New York on March 15, 2023 (Weather: Partly cloudy, 45°F, light winds) was spectacular."
```

**Use Cases:**
- **Journalists**: Add weather context to news articles
- **Writers**: Enhance stories with environmental details
- **Researchers**: Include weather data in reports
- **Bloggers**: Add context to travel or event posts
- **Students**: Include weather in historical research

## 10. Risk Mitigation

**Mitigation Strategies:**
